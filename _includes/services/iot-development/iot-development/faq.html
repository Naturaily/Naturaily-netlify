FAQ

How to develop IoT solution?

  • 01

    What is the function of IoT platforms?

    Its basic function is to connect devices or applications to another end point. An IoT platform can handle a significant amount of data gathered from devices, apps, websites and customers, as well as different types of sensors, giving responses in real-time.

    When choosing the best IoT platform, you should consider your company requirements for hardware, real-time access, reports, budget, development team and business model.

  • 02

    What are the types of IoT platforms nowadays?

    Top platforms nowadays are Google Cloud Platform, Particle, Sales IoT Cloud, ThingWorx, IBM Watson IoT, Amazon AWS IoT Core, Microsoft Azure IoT Suite, Samsung Artik Cloud, Oracle IoT and Cisco IoT Cloud.

  • 03

    Why is it worth starting from the discovery session?

    The discovery session is a deep dive into the client's business. It helps to understand the real problem that needs to be solved and establish the best way to do that. Workshops guarantee that both our client and us share the same vision of the solution we will be working on. After three days of working together, we come up with a plan on how to solve the stated problem in the cheapest, fastest, and most effective way.

    The discovery session’s outcome is documentation for project requirements, potential risks and ways to avoid them, as well as a basic roadmap and action plan with the project timeline and costs.

    The main goal of the discovery session is to find the best way to solve our clients' problems and help him or her succeed.

  • 04

    What is the Design Sprint methodology?

    It was developed at Google and written down by Jake Knapp and John Zeratsky in the form of a book titled “Sprint book”. In short, the Design Sprint is a five-day process for solving problems and testing new ideas. During workshops, the team works on a prototype and then shows it to customers to see their reaction.

  • 05

    When is a Proof of Concept needed?

    We recommend working on a PoC to each of our clients but, most of all, it’s recommended when the concept of the product has not been fully defined yet. It helps to identify use cases, notice potential bottlenecks and find the appropriate solution.
