    What is Design Thinking? {% svg /assets/images/btn-arrow.svg class="services-faq__orange-arrow" %}

    Design thinking is an iterative process of creating new and innovative ideas that solve problems. It seeks to understand users, make and challenge assumptions, redefine problems and create break-through solutions to prototype and test.

    The method consists of 5 phases:
    • Empathize
    • Define
    • Ideate
    • Prototype
    • Test
    It is not limited to any specific industry - it can be effective in design, technology or education, as it may be in other services.
  • 02

    Why do I need a prototype? {% svg /assets/images/btn-arrow.svg class="services-faq__orange-arrow" %}

    Prototyping your future product or service before the launch of the final version has several advantages.

    Making a prototype can highlight any conceptual or technical difficulties of the idea. Letting you test your product’s usability, navigation or information architecture also enhances the quality of your website or an app as a result.

    One of the advantages of using prototypes is reducing the risk of the project, as compared to a project in which prototyping was not included. It also lets you better estimate the number of work hours and resources needed.

    Finally, a prototype is a simulation of your future product, so it can help attract investors to your project before allocating any resources needed for implementation. Showing it to a bigger number of potential users will also provide you with a valuable feedback on your product’s usability.
